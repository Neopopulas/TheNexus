Overview

This page gives a short reference for the capital and each of the seven duchies.

High Magic

The most common type of magic in Kith. It is a practiced skill that must be studied and trained. Though not fully understood, some people are simply incapable of learning High Magic no matter how much work they put in, or how much they understand the theory, they lack the ability to grasp the fundamental forces that allow someone to use High Magic. High Magic is described by those with the spark to those that do not as being able to feel the vibrations of the world, and harnessing this energy, along with the structure and formula they have studied to perform the desired effect, but with the various types of wizards there are often variations.

High Magic is considered to be the most common and accepted type of magic in the known world. The reason for this is both its commonality as well as its position in society, High Magic is generally taught in academies and it is considered that only the learned and intelligent would be a practitioner, these academies are most available to the elite, providing wizards with a degree of social standing. Other magic - not including Divine magic - is generally considered a lesser tool, often used by lesser talents.

The High Magic Academy

The Academy of High Magic in the Royal City is the home of magic in the Kingdom. It is the largest of the schools and any self respecting prospective wizard would want to train there, but unfortunately few get the chance. Even practiced wizards desire to visit and learn at the Academy, or visit its vast library. The Academy is an enormous building set close to the center of the Royal City, next to the royal palace. It is towering square structure that extends hundreds of floors up and just as far below. It has few windows and no external doors. The only way to access the Academy is to known someone who already has access.


Bureaus

The High Magic Academy has numerous Bureaus around the kingdom. All capital cities have one, and many of the smaller towns also have some much smaller versions. The Bureaus are simply smaller versions of the main Academy, most are towers, but not all follow this theme, and all are founded and mostly funded by the main Academy in the Royal City. These smaller academies do their best to train up and coming wizards but their capabilities - and the talents of the teachers - vary wildly.


Practitioners

Practitioners of High magic are most often called Wizards, but they can be called Mages as well. Most Wizards come from - or have had access to - some form of money, be it simply a rich family or true nobility, there are few lucky types that gain some patron or find some other way to access the halls of a Bureau or another. A wizard’s talents and skills are honed at the Academies, and their specific choice in specialty will often be expressed early, but also influenced by their teachers.

Most lay-people consider Wizards to be intelligent, high class and somewhat dangerous, however the danger is tempered by the idea that wizards must be well trained, skilled and taught to use their powers wisely, to most people that don’t know better, anyone who does magic is a ‘wizard’, its not until they meet the person - and make a judgement call on the type of person they are - that they might try and figure out exactly what they are.

Most Small-Folk don’t care.

Low Magic

Low Magic is almost identical to High Magic, but where High Magic is considered to be refined and a matter of skill, Low Magic is considered to be a more crude, innate form of magic. People trust it less because it does not take skill and training, and ‘anyone’ can practice low magic by chance, many people will point to any use misuse of magic as ‘low’ magic. Those that practice Low Magic usual claim it is a natural part of themselves, like breathing or dancing and not something they most focus on the specifics of.


Practitioners

Those that practice Low Magic often find themselves practicing it on their own, not only due to the minor stigma of the name, but also because there is no school or skillset that is best trained to enhance it. People are often distrustful of those that practice Low Magic, so most don’t make a show of it. Practitioners are often called Sorcerers or Witches - usually in a derogatory way - and are considered to be the less trustworthy of magical users, perhaps due to the origin of their abilities.


Sources

The sources of Low magic are poorly understood, it is obvious that the ability to practice Low Magic runs in families, as does the type of Low Magic. There are many different types of Low Magic, though they are less common to see than High Magic.The origin of Low Magic is usually blamed on the introduction of non-human blood into the bloodline at some point in a families past, this places a stigma on the entire family even though there need be no evidence of influence in generations.

Divine Magic

Divine Magic is considered to be the most acceptable and beneficial magic in the kingdom. Everyone respects those that use Divine Magic as to gain access - almost - always requires a piety and religious training that puts the small-folk’s mind at ease. It comes directly from the Mother and Father - and thus ultimately from The Divine - and most of the small-folk consider this to be a blessing, and any that can practice such magic to be equally blessed.

Practitioners

Most practitioners are referred to as Priests or Clerics, they travel less than some other magic users and often stay close to their temple, or make pilgrimage to the Divine Temple in the Royal City. Some do indeed travel often with small groups or merchant caravans, they travel in an attempt to bring their specific belief to a wider audience, or if they believe they could be of aid with their specific skills.


Locations

Clerics find that their abilities are of greatest effect within the presence of the Divine - this could be either any temple of the Mother and Father, or in some situations, locations of even small shrines to their Aspects

Warlocks

Warlocks are rarely referred to directly. Most of the Small-Folk will mistake them for Wizards or Sorcerers, they just don’t know any better. Warlocks will often not make their magic choice a huge factor of their personality, there is no training required and no natural skill to hone. Warlocks gain their power from their connection to The Grey and their Patron that exists there. This could happen at any point in a persons life afte which their lives are forever changed.

Warlocks will often be able to continue their lives as normal before their pact - though few do - but most will find the pressure from their Patron, whether they know it or now, will push them towards specific tasks or goals that may not have interested them in the past.

Patrons

The Patrons of Warlocks are entities that exist in The Grey, these entities can take on innumerable forms and most have unknowable goals - whether because they refuse to divulge it, or because it is beyond human understanding. Those that study these things think that these entities have always existed, but gain power by the belief of humans - and specifically by how any people make pacts with them - and in recent years have become more common and more powerful.
