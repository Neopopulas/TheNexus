The Lost Forest

The Forest is to the far west of the Kingdom and borders Summerset and Gæzdænty Duchies. Its a mostly temperate forest - despite the weather in the rest of the kingdom - and is of a truly unknown size. Many people have tried to map the forest but maps never seem to stay accurate for long. Only the border of the forest has been accurately mapped as the two duchies will often push in as far as they can safety go for logging settlements.

Flora

The natural order in the Forest no longer seems to be as natural as it should be. It is said that the plants and trees seem to loom and menace people that entire the woods, people easily get lost among the trees and low underbrush, and almost every plant and fruiting bush is poisonous, hallucinogenic or does some other weird and dangerous thing.

Fauna

Much like the trees and bushes of the woods, the animals no longer seem to be normal either. Though at the edges of the woods, the animals remain more or less normal, the further you go the stranger things become. Not only are most animals in the forest significantly larger than they should be, from the insects to the mammals, there seems to be murderous hybrids and even animals of completely unknown origin. While not all are aggressive, their large size makes them predators to most humans.

Supernatural

There are also many supernatural elements of the forest. There is evidence that time passes differently in the forest, much more slowly than it does outside its borders. There are often stories of ‘ghosts’ within the woods, or spectres of some type, and while most are not hostile many have claimed that their apparitions whisper hateful things to them that no one else can hear. There seems to be a pervasive, low-lying fog within the woods as well, this fog sometimes creeps towards the edges where the loggers work and will usually drive them back to their homes until it clears. It is said if you spend too long in the fog you will be drawn further into the woods. It is also said to never enter the woods alone, or at night.

The Crown of Bones Mountains

The mountains in the north of the Kingdom borders several of the duchies, but most prominently the Whitecrest Duchy, which mines the mountains for metals, stone and precious gems. It is a dangerous and hostile environment, even if you don’t take into account its many dangerous inhabitants, the weather alone can be fatal to the unprepared.

Geography

Far to the north of the Kingdom is the Crown of Bones Mountains, named as such for the shattered, jagged peaks that curve around the northern point of the Kingdom. Inhospitable and deadly, the weather of the mountains is frigid and anyone traveling to the foot of the mountains must do so well prepared. The high winds are dangerous at any height, and there is little to no natural resources to forage. The foot of the mountains are riddled with caves and tunnels of unknown origin, all ancient by any estimation, these natural holds are often used by miners and guards as safe havens in the worst of the weather. Hundreds of mines - both open and tunnel - dot the landscape along the base of the mountains, few are willing to travel further into the mountains due to the risk of orcs and the weather getting the best of them.

Flora

Little grows in the mountains themselves, but the foothills are heavily wooded and often logged by the locals. These woods are sometimes said to hide orc raiders and tiny hidden outposts that they use to raid deeper into the Kingdom, these have never been found.

Fauna

The natural fauna of the foothills around the mountains seems normal - if sparse - for the location. Within the mountains themselves things change, there are many larger predators that roam the base of the mountains, including enormous wolves - which the Orcs have been known to ride - as well as cart-sized frost spiders which will ambush the unwary. It is also said that other, less mundane creatures haunt the higher peaks, including ridiculous rumours of an actual dragon at the peak.

Supernatural

The only obviously supernatural aspect of the mountains is the weather. Any normal mountain would have storms and dangerous weather, but the Crown of Bones mountain seems to have perpetually and exceptionally deadly weather at all times of the year. Snow is never seen to melt on its face and no matter the weather at the base, the mountain itself is always trapped in a glacial winter.

The Storm Sea

A barrier to the south, the Storm Sea is impassable. Often fished and traveled, the Storm Sea is treated with careful respect and only shallowly explored. Traveling up and down the coast may be save enough, and some island off the coast have been discovered and partially explored, the ocean is repeatedly proving how dangerous it is. The further you sail south the more dangerous it becomes. Sudden whirlpools, ship-wrecking storms and monsters pulling vessels down into the depths.

Geography

Along the cost seems relatively safe, though the Sea is always rough and there are few peaceful ports along the cost - primarily Black Bay in Kendle. Merchants and passenger vessels travel up and down the coast, sticking close for safety. Further out is rife with pirates, who willingly risk the dangers for the ability to dip in and out of the coastal waters to attack these vessels, often retreating back to the many small islands further out. The coastal waters are filled with coral reefs and barely hidden rocky islands, making travel the purview of experts of their trade.


Flora

Up and down the coast is choked with seaweed and other sea plants, making using the coast for anything other than deep sea fishing difficult. Some villages have grown up along the coast and often farm these coastal sea plants to export to nearby cities as animal feed. Unfortunately many of these sea plants are dangerous to humans and the profession is a dangerous one.

Fauna

The sea creatures along the coast of the Storm Sea are just as deadly as anywhere else. Sharks prowl up and down the coast and many poisonous animals inhabit the shallows. Some of these animals are farmed and sold along the interior of Kith. Deeper among the waves, the sharks grow exponentially and are joined by any number of dangerous creatures from sea serpents the size of warships and octopus large enough to drag the largest ships to the bottom of the ocean. It is said that even stranger monsters haunt the further depths, into the storms and perpetual fog of the deeper ocean it is said that truly immense creatures live.

Supernatural

It is said that almost everything in the Storm Sea is supernatural. Sailing more than a day from the coast is proof enough of that. Storms that are unnaturally powerful and much larger than should be possible. Currents that refuse to act in any sensible way, sea creatures that are far too large, far too smart and far too aggressive to be natural Many expeditions have been attempted to find other lands beyond the sea, but most have not returned and those that do say that they survived only by fleeing at the last moment.

The Thorn Wall

The Thorn wall is the eastern border of the Kingdom of Kith. It runs the entire length of the kingdom from the towering mountains in the north to the raging sea in the south. It is unclear how deep the wall is, but it is sufficient to keep out all attempts and all those that have penetrated deep enough into the wall to lose contact have never returned. In most places the wall is at least 100 feet tall and though reports say that it used to take up less space, it noticeably creeps further over the border, requiring constant pruning. The government no longer sponsors any attempt to breach the wall and instead acts to keep the wall contained in place and not allow it to progress any further into the kingdom.

Geography

The Thorn Wall takes up only a small part of the border, though it does creep further over the border day after day and it is the task of The Knights of the Thorn to keep the wall at bay and also prevent anything from within - or the other side - from breaching to the Kingdom’s side. Tangential to the Wall itself are the Outposts. The Outposts are stationed by the Knights to allow them to access as much of the wall as possible at short notice. These outposts are set as close to the wall as possible without triggering an attack. Many others live and work at these outposts that are not Knights themselves, workers - sometimes called Gardeners - are employed there and travel with the Knights when they work in the vicinity of the Wall, pruning it back and recovering any lost territory or overlooked buildings.

Flora

The most dangerous aspect of the Thorn Wall is is the Wall itself. Towering over the local area the Wall is a mass of twisted vines ranging from the size of a man’s pinky to the size of their entire body. These vines are covered in razor sharp thorns of equally varied size. Among these vines are a stunning array of flowers, many of them seeming to be some variety of rose. From a distance the ever-blooming wall is a marvel to view, a towering wall of permanently vibrant roses backed by dark green foliage, swaying in the breeze. Up close the wall seems to be something entirely different, when close enough to view the wall properly it is obvious that the vines are always in movement, writhing and twisting around themselves. If a living creature moves too close to the wall, the vines will lash out with shocking speed, seeking to grasp and drag the creature into the wall itself. The vines are incredibly strong and difficult to damage. From time to time there have been sightings of ‘tunnels’ in the wall, it is forbidden to enter these tunnels and the Knights will appear as soon as they hear of one in an attempt to close it, or at least guard it until it does.

Fauna

There is little in the way of Fauna around the wall, anything living that comes close enough is grabbed and dragged into the wall, never to be seen again, even birds that accidentally fly too close will be snatched out of the sky and killed However there are some rare creatures that have appeared from the wall itself. Very occasionally mysterious and unknown animals have been found - dead - on the outside of the wall. These animals have been studied but seem to match no known animals of the Kingdom.

Supernatural

The supernatural aspects of the Thorn Wall are obvious, it seems to be living, if not sentient, and hostile to living creatures. It seems to want to advance inwards towards the kingdom in a slow and methodical way. Many magical attempts have been made to divine its purpose and origin and have come up short. Occasional attempts to study the ‘tunnels’ that appear in the wall - and even enter them - have been met with failure as well, often times the tunnels will close themselves without warning, trapping anyone within them.
