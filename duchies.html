This page gives a short reference for the capital and each of the seven duchies.

The Capital

The Capital

The Gaezdaenty Duchy

House Head: Tsomak Ogoev
Heir: Vano Ogoev
Location: North-West
Capital: Hannal

The small Duchy of Gæzdænty. Comprising one large city and several hamlets. The capital city of Hannal benefits from its proximity to this forest, as the creatures provide resources, sport but are also a considerable threat. The city has a strong guard to defend against threats emerging from the forest, and the local temple attracts priests with healing magic, providing good healthcare. Though lacking in culture or military might, Hannal prospers on forest resources and trade. The Duke and his family are well-educated, but most commoners receive little schooling, instead joining the temple or guard. Production in the city is limited, depending on forest goods and trade. Entertainment is sporadic, with the occasional troupe passing through. Overall, Hannue is a moderately prosperous city, sustained by its relationship with the mysterious forest and the creatures that dwell within.

Capital City Hannue is a well defended city and always has been. The many remnants of ancient walls, torn down to build houses and markets and parks as the town grew every larger, proof that no matter how the city grows, it always grows within the walls the surround it.

To the west of the city is the dark forests, filled with giant, monstrous animals, mists that steal your mind, a place where time is fallible. The majority of the population take their income from the resources of the forest, in one way or another.

In the center of the city, on a large hill is the sprawling mansion that the Duke calls home, growing almost as fast as the city that surrounds it.

The Kokev-Kesav Duchy

House Head: Akhsar Kokev-Kesev
Heir: Mikhal Kokev-Kesav
Location: West

The small Kokev Duchy is trapped between the powerful Summerset Duchy to the south and the Gaezdaenty Duchy to the north. Lacking land and influence, the Duchy relies on the market towns along the main highway connecting the two larger Duchies for its wealth. With farms to the south and unique animal goods from the north, many travelers stop in Kokev lands to buy and sell. Though weak, the Duchy profits from its strategic location along a major trade route.

Despite its limitations, the Kokev Duchy has managed to maintain its independence for generations. The current Duke, Akhsar Kokev-Kesav, governs from the city of Raemord. Though the Duchy lacks military strength, its location in a heavily trafficked and populated area deters aggression from its more powerful neighbors.

The Duchy's economy depends almost entirely on trade and commerce. Farmers in the south supply grains and vegetables, which traders transport north. Animal products like fur, leather, and meat from the northern forests travel south. The Duchy collects tolls and tariffs on all goods moving through its lands.

Though the Duchy lacks natural resources or a strong military, its strategic position on a major trade route has allowed it to prosper. By facilitating commerce between its neighbors, the Kokav Duchy has secured its autonomy despite its weaknesses.

The Summerset Duchy

House Head: Lucien Gael
Heir: Lucien Gael II
Location: South-west

The Duchy nestles in the southwestern Kingdom, bordering a strip of sea to the south and The Forest the west. Dotted with small villages devoted to farming, the Duchy is the breadbasket of the kingdom and, with one other, feeds most of the realm. The Gael family has ruled the Duchy for generations from Summerset Manor. In recent decades, the Gaels have boosted harvests, becoming one of the Seven most powerful families and often influencing the King. Though somewhat closed off, the Gaels are popular for guarding roads against dangers from The Forest. They hire mercenaries to prevent the worst threats from stretching into their lands.

The Gaels rule from the hilltop Summerset Manor, its palatial sprawl visible for miles around. Their lands have prospered for a century, harvests booming to make them one of the realm’s most powerful families—and most closed off. Yet they guard their people against the Forest’s dangers, hiring mercenaries to patrol the roads and repel the worst threats.

Though aloof, the Gaels are popular for shielding farms and villages. Most peasants accept the family’s rule, trusting their efforts to prevent calamity. Only a few whisper of the Gaels’ secretive, unchanging ways, or chafe under their influence over the King.

Still, the Duchy remains peaceful. Its people farm, trade, and live simply, knowing the Gaels keep darker forces at bay. The Forest looms, but its shadow falls elsewhere. The Gaels stand as a bulwark, allowing their lands to thrive through a prosperous quietude.

The Wingmore Duchy

House Head: Lasho Hawkins
Heir: Garridan Hawkins
Location: South
Capital City: The Port City of Kendle

Stretching across the south of the kingdom, the wide Wingmore Duchy encompasses most of the southern coast. Founder first be Selmi Wingmore it has passed through several hands before falling, several generations ago, to the Hawkins. It is said that the Ocean is cursed, and the position of the Duchy - especially the Whitespray Keep - is cursed as well, the curse following whomever oversees its domain The Hawkins Dynasty, masters of the sea, have rules for generations now and their fishing fleet helps feed the kingdom; they also provide transport along the southern coast.

Often in the past, the Hawkins sent expedition forces into the Grey Sea. Very few returned, but those who did brought back wild and unbelievable tales. Still, occasionally the rich, foolish, or both, set out to explore uncharted waters. The Hawkins rule Kendle and the duchy from their palace, high on the cliffs overlooking the sea. Kendle produces little itself, but its docks and markets overflow with goods from across the world.

Entertainers of all kinds fill the streets and inns, catering to visitors and residents alike. Some who seek adventure or fortune embark from Kendle's docks to explore the uncharted reaches of the Grey Sea. Most never return.

The Larsen Duchy

House Head: Kasper Larsen
Heir: Aage Larsen
Location: South-East

In the southeast, the Larsen Duchy struggles under pressure from both Adriaan Vosmeijer to the north and the dangerous Thorn Wall to the east. Primarily forested, the duchy has a few larger towns and an array of hunting lodges and foresters. These industries make up most of House Larsen’s income. Kasper Larsen is notably absent from his responsibilities for much of the year, spending most of his time in the capital attempting—with little success—to gain favor with the Crown.

The capital city benefits from the Duchy's abundant natural resources. Duke Larsen's mismanagement and absenteeism have left the Duchy vulnerable, dependent on mercenaries and exposed to external pressures. Should the Duke fail to overcome his political missteps, Larsen may face a precarious future.

The Vosmijer Duchy

House Head: Adriaan Vosmeijer
Heir: Joris Vosmeijer
Location: North-East

In the North-East of the Kingdom is the Vosmeijer Duchy. Named for Teun Vosmeijer, the first to lay claim to these lands, they have remained in the Vosmeijer family ever since. Famed for their skill at arms they primarily export skilled soldiers and armsmen across the kingdom.

Most well known for their Knights, the Knights of the Thorn are an order of knights that were founded generations ago to protect the Kingdom from the Thorn Wall and its inhabitants. Ostensibly sponsored by the Crown it is mostly funded and maintained by the Vosmeijer Duchy and as such Joris Vosmeijer maintains significant control over its actions. This has caused some amount of tension with their neighbors to the south as their sponsorship by the crown allows them to operate up and down the eastern wall, which often puts them within the boarders of the Larsen Duchy.

The Whitecrest Duchy

House Head: Arjan Zoer
Heir: None
Location: North

Situated far to the north, the Whitecrest Duchy is small in people but large in land. One of the largest Duchies in land mass it is mostly made up of inhospitable frozen land just south of the mountains. Most of the land that is used is used for mining purposes and is often harassed by monsters from the frozen mountains.

Precious metals and gems, as well as the raw stone used throughout the Kingdom is found in the lands of the Whitecrest Duchy. Ruled by the house of Zoer for the last eight generations, the previous house of Toen was removed after being unable to protect its lands from the trolls and giants to the north. None have been seen in generations and the house of Zoer have ruled in relative peace since.

The people of the Whitecrest Duchy are hardy and tough people, almost exclusively miners or craftsmen with a large contingent of armed guards to protect the sites it makes for a very rough and barren land.
