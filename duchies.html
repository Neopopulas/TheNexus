Kingdom Overview

The Duchies

The kingdom is divided between the capital and seven duchies, each ruled by old houses, practical interests, inherited grudges, and the occasional noble with more ambition than sense.

Overview

This page gives a short reference for the capital and each of the seven duchies. Each entry can cover the duchy’s ruling family, geography, major settlements, local culture, military strength, trade, conflicts, and anything else players are likely to care about before they accidentally offend someone important.

The Capital

Replace this with the capital’s overview. Mention where it sits, who rules from it, what it controls, and what people from the duchies think of it.

This is also a good place to note the royal court, major temples, markets, academies, fortifications, old districts, and any political tensions that make visiting the capital more interesting than simply buying shoes.

Duchy One

Replace this with the first duchy’s overview. Give the duchy’s landscape, ruling house, main settlement, and general character.

You might also include what the duchy produces, what it fears, who it rivals, and what kind of problems adventurers tend to find there.

Duchy Two

Replace this with the second duchy’s overview. This could be a coastal duchy, a trade-heavy region, a frontier march, a religious centre, or whatever fits your map.

A few paragraphs are enough. The goal is to give players a strong first impression without making them read a council tax report with swords in it.

Duchy Three

Replace this with the third duchy’s overview. Focus on the things that make it distinct from the others: terrain, culture, wealth, rulers, dangers, or its role in the kingdom.

If it has a current crisis, put that here. Succession trouble, banditry, magical blight, border raids, religious unrest. Nobles need hobbies, apparently.

Duchy Four

Replace this with the fourth duchy’s overview. Describe its mood and identity first, then add the practical details.

Good reference details include the capital town, ruling family, major roads, exports, old ruins, famous battles, and nearby threats.

Duchy Five

Replace this with the fifth duchy’s overview. If this region has a strong visual identity, mention it early: wheat fields, black cliffs, pine forests, marshland, white roads, red stone, or whatever makes it easy to picture.

This is also a good place to note how outsiders talk about the duchy, even if the locals strongly disagree and keep a list.

Duchy Six

Replace this with the sixth duchy’s overview. Keep it focused on what players need: who has power, what matters there, and what might pull characters into trouble.

A duchy can be peaceful on paper and still be full of old debts, hidden shrines, smuggling routes, lost heirs, or one very annoying bridge dispute.

Duchy Seven

Replace this with the seventh duchy’s overview. Since this is the last entry, it may help to make it especially distinct: the borderland, the old heartland, the wild duchy, the richest duchy, or the place everyone politely avoids.

End with the duchy’s current concern or unresolved tension. That gives the entry a hook instead of making it sit there like a labelled jar.
